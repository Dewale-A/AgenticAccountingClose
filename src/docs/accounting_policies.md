# Accounting Policies Manual

## Overview

This document outlines the accounting policies for the organization. All policies comply with US GAAP (ASC standards) and are designed to support accurate financial reporting in a SOX-compliant environment. Changes to these policies require CFO approval and must be documented with an effective date.

---

## 1. Revenue Recognition (ASC 606)

### Policy Statement

Revenue is recognized when control of goods or services transfers to the customer. The five-step model applies to all revenue transactions:

1. **Identify the contract** with the customer
2. **Identify performance obligations** in the contract
3. **Determine the transaction price** (including variable consideration)
4. **Allocate the transaction price** to performance obligations
5. **Recognize revenue** when (or as) each performance obligation is satisfied

### Product Revenue

- Product revenue is recognized at the point of delivery when the customer takes physical possession and title transfers.
- For FOB shipping point terms, revenue is recognized when the product ships.
- For FOB destination terms, revenue is recognized when the product arrives at the customer location.
- Returns and allowances are estimated based on historical return rates and recorded as a reduction of revenue at the time of sale.

### Service Revenue

- Service revenue is recognized over time as services are performed.
- For fixed-fee contracts, revenue is recognized on a straight-line basis over the service period.
- For milestone-based contracts, revenue is recognized when milestones are achieved and accepted.
- Unbilled service revenue is recorded as a contract asset. Amounts billed but not yet earned are recorded as deferred revenue.

### Period-End Cutoff

- All revenue transactions must be recorded in the period in which the performance obligation is satisfied.
- The Revenue team must complete the revenue cutoff analysis by close day 2.
- Transactions within 3 business days of period end require supporting documentation confirming delivery date.

## 2. Expense Accruals

### Policy Statement

Expenses are recognized in the period in which they are incurred, regardless of when payment is made. This ensures expenses are matched to the revenue they help generate (matching principle).

### Accrual Procedures

- **Recurring accruals**: Salaries, benefits, rent, and utilities are accrued based on the standard monthly amounts. Payroll accrues from the last payroll date through the end of the month.
- **Non-recurring accruals**: Professional fees, project costs, and one-time expenses are accrued based on invoices received, contracts, or management estimates.
- **Threshold**: Expenses below $1,000 may be recorded on a cash basis for immaterial items. Items above $1,000 must be properly accrued.
- **Reversal**: Recurring accruals are reversed on the first day of the following month. Non-recurring accruals are reversed when the actual invoice is received and recorded.

### Supporting Documentation

- All accruals above $10,000 require a memo documenting the basis for the estimate.
- Accruals above $50,000 require manager approval before recording.
- Year-end accruals require additional scrutiny and controller sign-off.

## 3. Prepaid Amortization

### Policy Statement

Prepaid expenses are amortized on a straight-line basis over the period of benefit. This ensures expenses are recognized in the periods that benefit from the prepayment.

### Procedures

- **Insurance**: Annual insurance premiums are amortized monthly (1/12 per month). The full premium is recorded as a prepaid asset upon payment.
- **Rent**: Prepaid rent is amortized over the lease term. For month-to-month leases, the prepayment is expensed in the month it covers.
- **Software subscriptions**: Annual software licenses are amortized monthly over the subscription period.
- **Other prepaids**: Any payment for future benefit exceeding $5,000 is recorded as prepaid and amortized over the benefit period.

### Reconciliation

- The Prepaid schedule is reconciled monthly.
- New prepaids are added with the vendor, amount, start date, end date, and monthly amortization amount.
- Fully amortized prepaids are removed from the schedule after the final month.

## 4. Depreciation Policy

### Policy Statement

Property and equipment are depreciated using the straight-line method over the estimated useful life of each asset. This allocates the cost of the asset evenly across the periods that benefit from its use.

### Useful Lives

| Asset Category | Useful Life | Salvage Value |
|---------------|-------------|---------------|
| Buildings | 39 years | 10% |
| Building improvements | 15 years | 0% |
| Furniture and fixtures | 7 years | 0% |
| Computer equipment | 3 years | 0% |
| Vehicles | 5 years | 10% |
| Manufacturing equipment | 10 years | 5% |

### Capitalization Threshold

- Assets costing $5,000 or more are capitalized and depreciated.
- Assets below $5,000 are expensed in the period of purchase.
- Costs for repairs and maintenance are expensed as incurred.
- Costs that extend the useful life or add functionality are capitalized.

### Monthly Calculation

- Depreciation is calculated monthly using the formula: (Cost minus Salvage Value) / (Useful Life in Months).
- The depreciation journal entry (DR: Depreciation Expense, CR: Accumulated Depreciation) is posted automatically by the fixed asset module.
- New assets placed in service mid-month use the half-month convention.

## 5. Lease Accounting (ASC 842)

### Policy Statement

All leases with terms greater than 12 months are recorded on the balance sheet. This policy applies to real estate leases, equipment leases, and vehicle leases.

### Classification

- **Operating leases**: The right-of-use (ROU) asset and lease liability are recorded on the balance sheet. Lease expense is recognized on a straight-line basis over the lease term.
- **Finance leases**: The ROU asset is amortized separately from the interest on the lease liability. Front-loaded expense pattern.

### Short-Term Lease Exemption

Leases with terms of 12 months or less (including renewal options the company is reasonably certain to exercise) are expensed on a straight-line basis without recording an ROU asset.

### Monthly Close Procedures

- Verify all active leases are in the lease schedule.
- Record monthly lease expense (operating) or amortization plus interest (finance).
- Reconcile the ROU asset and lease liability balances to the lease schedule.
- New leases signed during the period must be added within 5 business days of execution.

## 6. Intercompany Transactions

### Policy Statement

All intercompany transactions are recorded at arm's length pricing and eliminated in consolidation. Intercompany balances must net to zero after elimination.

### Procedures

- Intercompany invoices are processed through the intercompany module with matching entries in both entities.
- Intercompany balances are reconciled monthly between all entities.
- Differences must be investigated and resolved before the close is finalized.
- Transfer pricing documentation is maintained by the Tax department.

### Elimination Entries

- Revenue and expense eliminations are posted as part of the consolidation process.
- Balance sheet eliminations (intercompany receivables/payables) are posted monthly.
- Any unresolved intercompany differences are escalated to the Controller.

## 7. Period-End Cutoff Procedures

### Policy Statement

Proper cutoff ensures transactions are recorded in the correct accounting period. This is critical for accurate financial reporting and is a key area of focus for external auditors.

### Revenue Cutoff

- No revenue is recorded for goods not yet shipped or services not yet performed as of month end.
- Sales orders entered on the last day of the month require shipping documentation confirming dispatch before period end.
- The revenue cutoff report is run on close day 1 and reviewed by the Revenue Accounting Manager.

### Expense Cutoff

- All invoices received before the close date are recorded in the current period.
- Invoices received after close but relating to the current period are accrued.
- The AP team runs the expense cutoff report by close day 2 showing invoices received in the first 5 business days of the new month that relate to the prior period.

### Cash Cutoff

- Bank reconciliations capture all transactions through the last business day of the month.
- Wire transfers initiated on the last business day are recorded based on the value date, not the initiation date.
- The Treasury team confirms all cash transactions are properly dated by close day 3.
